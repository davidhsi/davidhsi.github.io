Predicting House Prices Project

Problem Statement: The housing buyers in Ames, Iowa want their own estimate of a potential house rather than the realtor’s estimate which is likely higher than the actual price. We wanted to create a prediction model to predict potential housing prices in Ames, Iowa based on the different variables the data provides.
  • Stakeholders: Home buyers, home sellers, and realtors
  • Variable selection was a big part of the project, we had to choose the most important predictors in predicting prices.
  • Looked at the predictors with the highest correlation to the target variable and investigated issues of multicollinearity with VIF
    • Inital model (slide #1): Multiple linear regression model with predictors OverallQual, GrLivArea, and GarageCars and target variable 'SalePrice'
      • R^2 of 73.2% and RMSE of $37,558
    • Improving the model (slides #2 and #3 ):
      • Removed high leverage points
      • Log transformed 'SalePrice' and Square Root transformed 'GrLivArea'
      • Interaction between 'OverallQual' and 'GrLivArea'
  • Final Model (slide #4) gave a R^2 of 78.0% (6.5% improvement from inital model) and RMSE of $33,643 (11.6% decrease)
